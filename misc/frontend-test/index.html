WLC Slow Control DESIGN PREVIEW

Three layout concepts for the local dashboard · DUNE High Bay clean room

Three ways to evolve the local slow-control dashboard, all built in your existing house style (Yale Blue header, Wong colorblind-safe traces, Arial, red/orange/green status). Each adds the 5 distributed temp/humidity sensors and a login/lock. Toggle light/dark on each page. These are mockups with synthetic data — no real readings, nothing connected.

Format A

Minimal-Change Evolution

Your dashboard, untouched — same 6-card row and chart stack. The single env chart becomes two overlaid 5-sensor charts with status chips. Lock chip added to the header.

● Adoptable tomorrow
Format B

Overview + Drill-Down

Room state up top, then a tidy 5-panel small-multiples grid. Click any sensor to expand its full history. Sectioned (Cleanliness / Environment / Health) with a Coldbox system switch.

● Recommended balance
Format C

Spatial-First (CERN-style)

A floor-plan of the High Bay is the centerpiece — sensors are status-colored markers you click to read. Cleanliness panels around it, plus a reserved controls rail for future HV.

● Most ambitious
Files live in frontend-test/. Open any one in a browser, or start a tiny server: python3 -m http.server 5700 -d frontend-test then visit localhost:5700.
